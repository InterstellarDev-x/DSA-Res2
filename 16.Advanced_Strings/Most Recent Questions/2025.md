## Trend Analysis — 2025

- **Longest Palindromic Substring** held its place as the single most-asked string problem across the board.
- Google escalated **Rabin-Karp** problems: Longest Duplicate Substring and Distinct Echo Substrings both surfaced at L5, with interviewers explicitly probing **double hashing** and collision handling.
- KMP-based problems (Shortest Palindrome, Repeated Substring Pattern) spread from Google into Microsoft's medium rotation.
- Anagram sliding-window questions stayed steady at Amazon and Adobe — the reliable "do you know fixed-window frequency" check.
