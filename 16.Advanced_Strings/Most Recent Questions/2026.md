## Trend Analysis — 2026 (YTD)

- **Longest Palindromic Substring** remains the universal staple — present at every company tracked, three years running. Expand-around-center is accepted; interviewers occasionally ask for the O(n) Manacher follow-up.
- Google's **KMP hard differentiators** (Shortest Palindrome, Longest Happy Prefix) and **Rabin-Karp** (Longest Duplicate Substring) continue to define the senior bar; double hashing is now an *expected* mention, not bonus.
- `strStr` stays the universal easy entry point — almost every company opens with it or uses it as a phone-screen warm-up.
- No new problem types have displaced the core nine; the topic is stable, with difficulty differentiation coming from follow-up depth (collisions, O(n) palindromes, periodicity proofs) rather than new problems.

### Three-year takeaways
1. **Longest Palindromic Substring is the universal string staple** — master expand-around-center cold.
2. **KMP-based problems (Shortest Palindrome, Happy Prefix) are Google's hard differentiators** — know the `s + '#' + reverse(s)` derivation.
3. **Rabin-Karp (Longest Duplicate Substring) is rising at Google L5** — binary search on length plus double hashing is the expected answer.
