# Greedy Algorithms — 2025 Interview Questions

## Overview
2025 was a landmark year for greedy interview questions. **Jump Game II and Gas Station appeared at every major company** (Amazon, Google, Microsoft, Goldman Sachs) in at least one round. Partition Labels became Google's signature greedy question. Heap-based greedy (Meeting Rooms II) became a near-mandatory topic for senior (L5/L6) rounds.

## 2025 Key Trends

### Trend 1: Jump Game II and Gas Station — Universal Presence
Both problems appeared at **every major company** (Amazon, Google, Microsoft, Goldman Sachs) in 2025. If you only study two greedy problems for 2026, study these two.

### Trend 2: Verbal Proof Required at Senior Levels
Google, Goldman Sachs, and Amazon senior rounds (L5+, SDE-2+) began explicitly asking:
- "Why does your greedy choice work?"
- "Can you sketch an exchange argument?"
- "What's the minimum counterexample if this greedy were wrong?"

### Trend 3: Heap-Greedy Rising
Meeting Rooms II and Task Scheduler graduated from "nice-to-know" to "expected knowledge" for L4/L5 roles. The combo of PriorityQueue + greedy sort is now standard.

### Trend 4: Amazon OA Format Shift
Amazon's 70-minute, 2-question OA format in 2025 often combined one greedy problem with one DP problem, testing candidates' ability to quickly identify which paradigm applies.
