## 2026 Emerging Trends (Through Q2)

### Trend 1: "Why Greedy?" Verbal Proofs Now Standard at L4+
In 2026, even L4-level Google and L5-level Amazon interviews routinely ask:
- "Prove this greedy choice is optimal."
- "What's the exchange argument here?"
- "When would greedy fail? Construct a counterexample."

**Recommendation:** Practice 2-minute verbal proofs for Non-overlapping Intervals and Gas Station.

### Trend 2: Non-overlapping Intervals Surging
After being primarily a Microsoft/Amazon question in 2024-2025, Non-overlapping Intervals now appears frequently at Google (Q2 2026). The exchange-argument proof is explicitly requested.

### Trend 3: Greedy vs DP Decision-Making Tested
2026 interviewers increasingly ask: "Could this be solved with DP? Is greedy strictly better, or just cleaner?"
- **Jump Game:** Both greedy O(n) and DP O(n²) work; greedy is better.
- **Candy:** Greedy (two-pass) is O(n); DP formulation exists but is also O(n) with same space.
- **Task Scheduler:** Both formula (greedy) and heap simulation (greedy) work; DP over-engineers.

### Trend 4: Combination Problems Emerging
2026 started seeing "combo" problems that layer greedy on top of another pattern:
- "Jump Game on a 2D grid" (greedy + BFS)
- "Meeting Rooms II with cancellation" (greedy + priority queue + lazy deletion)
- "Gas Station with variable tank capacity" (greedy + binary search)
