## Trend Analysis

Across 2025, the same three families that defined 2024 remained the backbone of Binary Tree interviews:

- **LCA variants (236, 1123):** Standard LCA (236) continued to appear at Amazon, Microsoft, and Adobe, while Google leaned into the harder LCA of Deepest Leaves (1123). The pattern remains a dependable filter for recursive thinking.
- **Path sum family (112 / 113 / 124):** Path Sum II (113) was especially common this year across Google, Goldman Sachs, and as a follow-up elsewhere, with Maximum Path Sum (124) still anchoring Amazon's harder rounds.
- **Serialize / Deserialize (297):** Held its place as a go-to design problem at Amazon, Google, and Adobe.

The notable 2025 shift was the rise of **Tree DP** problems — House Robber III (337), Binary Tree Cameras (968), and Distribute Coins (979) appeared more frequently, signaling interviewers' growing interest in post-order accumulation and state-on-node reasoning. Level-order variants also broadened beyond plain BFS into width and next-pointer follow-ups.
