## Trend Analysis

Three years of data confirm a remarkably stable core, and 2026 sharpens where the field is heading:

- **LCA variants (236, 1123):** Still ubiquitous — standard LCA (236) appears at Amazon, Microsoft, and Goldman Sachs, while Google continues to push LCA of Deepest Leaves (1123). This family has been a constant across all three years and all five companies.
- **Path sum family (112 / 113 / 124):** Unchanged in importance, spanning the easy gate (112) through the hard Maximum Path Sum (124). Path Sum II (113) remains the most-asked middle-tier variant.
- **Serialize / Deserialize (297):** Appears at every company tracked here in 2026, cementing its status as the canonical tree-design question across the full 2024–2026 window.

**Emerging focus for 2026:** the clearest forward signal is the shift toward problems that blend trees with graph-style and DP reasoning. Tree-DP questions (House Robber III, Binary Tree Cameras, Distribute Coins) and reachability/restructuring problems (All Nodes Distance K — 863, Delete Nodes and Return Forest — 1110) are now featured prominently at Amazon, Google, and Microsoft. Candidates preparing this year should master post-order state propagation and the trick of converting a tree into an undirected graph for distance queries, alongside the durable LCA / path-sum / serialize core.
