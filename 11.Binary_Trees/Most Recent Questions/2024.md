## Trend Analysis

Looking back over 2024, three problem families dominated Binary Tree rounds across every company surveyed:

- **LCA variants (236, 1123):** The classic Lowest Common Ancestor (236) was an almost universal screening question, appearing at Amazon, Google, and Microsoft. Interviewers favored it for its clean recursion and easy follow-ups.
- **Path sum family (112 / 113 / 124):** Path Sum and its variants showed up consistently, from the easy gating version (112) at Microsoft to the hard Maximum Path Sum (124) at Google. These tested candidates' comfort with return-value propagation in recursion.
- **Serialize / Deserialize (297):** A reliable design-flavored question, surfacing at Amazon, Google, and Adobe onsites. It rewarded a clean, well-specified encoding scheme.

In retrospect, 2024 set the baseline: a stable core of LCA, path sum, and serialize/deserialize that companies returned to year after year. The traversal and level-order questions rounded out the rest, typically used as warm-ups before the harder recursion-heavy problems.
