## 2026 Trend Analysis (year to date)

- **The two pillars are unmoved.** Number of Islands and Course Schedule remain the most consistently asked graph questions across every company — three straight years as the universal baseline.
- **Minimax Dijkstra now mainstream.** What was a 2025 uptick (*Path With Minimum Effort*, *Swim in Rising Water*) is now a standard mid-level expectation at Google and Amazon. Candidates are expected to modify the relaxation to `max(pathSoFar, edge)` without hints.
- **Union-Find firmly entrenched at Amazon & Goldman.** *Number of Islands II* and *Accounts Merge* are routine; interviewers probe path compression + union by rank explicitly.
- **Alien Dictionary unretired at Google.** Still the canonical graph-modeling question, with the prefix-conflict case as the discriminator.
- **Tarjan bridges stable at Google L5.** Critical Connections persists in senior loops.
